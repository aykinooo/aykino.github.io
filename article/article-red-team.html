Red Team: Introduction to Offensive Security

Feb 19, 2026

Red Teaming simulates real adversaries to measure how well an organization can detect, respond to, and recover from realistic attacks. The goal is not only exploitation, but full security validation across people, process, and technology.

Scope and Rules

A proper engagement starts with strict scope: approved targets, testing windows, escalation contacts, and explicit forbidden actions. This keeps testing legal, controlled, and useful for defenders.

Typical Workflow

The offensive cycle usually includes reconnaissance, initial access, privilege escalation, lateral movement, and persistence testing. Each phase should generate evidence that defenders can replay in detection tooling.

What Makes It Valuable

Red Team exercises uncover blind spots that vulnerability scans miss: weak identity controls, poor network segmentation, alert fatigue, and incident response gaps under pressure.

Takeaway

The best Red Team report is actionable. Findings should include exploitation path, business impact, and concrete mitigation steps for Blue Team and engineering teams.
